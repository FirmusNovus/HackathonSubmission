# Task

What does this company do. A pan-EU pseudonymous legal advice platform that uses EBSI/EUDI verifiable credentials to cryptographically prove every lawyer on the platform is a real, currently-admitted member of an EU bar in their stated jurisdiction and specialty, while clients are verified once at the platform's regulated identity-escrow layer and remain pseudonymous to the lawyer through ZK-attested AML and conflict-of-interest checks. The product is delivered in three tiers — anonymous legal information, pseudonymous-but-credentialed advice with sealed-but-unsealable identity, and standard fully-identified engagement when a matter escalates — bridging the gap between the unverified open internet and the friction of traditional firm onboarding, with cross-border matching as the wedge (e.g. a Spanish startup founder finding a vetted Spanish lawyer admitted in Germany without uploading documents to a directory site).

How does it make money. Primary revenue is a take rate of 10–20% on legal fees that flow through the platform's smart-contract escrow, charged at milestone release rather than at signup so it scales with actual work performed; a verified lawyer subscription (€50–200/month) gives premium matching, multi-jurisdiction badging, and analytics; enterprise modules sell into corporate legal departments and compliance teams that need cross-border counsel for GDPR, employment, sanctions, or immigration with a per-seat or per-matter fee; and a longer-term licensing line offers the credential-verifier and ZK conflict-check infrastructure as a B2B SaaS to other regulated marketplaces (medical, accounting, financial advisory) that face the same "verify the professional, not the client" problem.

How much money could it make. The EU legal services market is forecast at USD 265.67 billion in 2026 and projected to reach USD 310.68 billion by 2031 at a 3.18% CAGR GlobeNewswire (https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/01/30/3229424/28124/en/Europe-Legal-Services-Market-2026-2031-Cybersecurity-Threats-Escalate-for-European-Law-Firms-Amid-Rising-Data-Breach-Risks.html) , with the B2C slice alone at USD 61.62 billion in 2025 growing at 5.65% CAGR to USD 81.10 billion by 2030 Mordor Intelligence (https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/europe-b2c-legal-services-market) and EU legal tech at USD 6.81 billion in 2026 expanding at 10.78% CAGR toward USD 15.45 billion by 2034 Market Data Forecast (https://www.marketdataforecast.com/market-reports/europe-legal-tech-market) ; a defensible serviceable market — cross-border, SME, and digitally-deliverable B2C matters — is roughly €25–40B, and capturing 0.25–0.5% of that as platform GMV in year 5 means €60–200M flowing through escrow annually, translating to €10–35M in net revenue at a 15% take rate, with legal-tech comparables (Clio, Ironclad, Luminance, LawVu) trading at 6–12× ARR putting a credible €100–400M valuation range on a successful execution; the asymmetric upside is becoming the reference platform consuming the EUDI Wallet as it rolls out across all 27 member states by end of 2026, which is regulatory tailwind no incumbent has positioned for.

Tech stack. EBSI's Trusted Issuers Registry and DID Registry (read-only, REST) for verifying lawyer practising-certificate VCs, with the @cef-ebsi/verifiable-credential and @cef-ebsi/verifiable-presentation libraries handling JWT-VC validation; OID4VP for client PID presentation from EUDI-conformant wallets; Solidity smart contracts on an Ethereum L2 (Base or Arbitrum for low-cost engagement and escrow flows, with mainnet only used for high-value attestations) using EAS (Ethereum Attestation Service) for verified-lawyer and verified-client attestations and ERC-5564 stealth addresses for per-engagement client identifiers; ZK circuits in Noir or Circom for the conflict-check protocol (proving non-membership in a lawyer's encrypted-client Merkle tree) and BBS+/SD-JWT for selective disclosure of credential attributes; XMTP or Waku for end-to-end-encrypted lawyer-client messaging with on-chain hash commitments for audit trails; QES integration via a Qualified Trust Service Provider partner (Namirial, Universign, D-Trust) for legally-binding signed agreements; backend in TypeScript/Node with a Postgres + Redis stack hosted in EU regions for GDPR posture, frontend Next.js with wagmi/viem for wallet interaction; commercial KYC fallback (Fractal ID or Sumsub) for users without EUDI Wallet coverage during the 2026–2027 transition; observability via OpenTelemetry; CI/CD on GitHub Actions with Foundry for contract testing and Slither/MythX for static analysis.
